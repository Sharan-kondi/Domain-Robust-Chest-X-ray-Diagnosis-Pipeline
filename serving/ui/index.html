Part I

The Invisible Medical Border

When a deep learning model is trained at a major research hospital, it excels. But hospital systems use different digital radiography scanners (GE, Siemens, Philips), contrasting image processing pipelines, and serve varying demographics. This results in **Covariate Shift**—an invisible border where highly-trained models silently fail, producing incorrect diagnostics when deployed at a new site.

Clinical Aspect

A model trained solely on high-contrast US hospital images will misinterpret the lower-contrast images of a field clinic, mistaking machine noise for active pathology like pneumonia or consolidations.
Part II

Aligning the Covariance

To cross this border, Lumen CXR utilizes **Correlation Alignment (CORAL)**. During model training, the network is optimized not just to label pathologies, but to align the second-order feature statistics (covariance) between the training dataset and target hospital systems. The neural network learns to discard scanner-specific patterns and focus purely on clinical anatomy.

Mathematical Alignment

By penalizing the distance between source and target feature covariance matrices, features represent clinical geometry rather than scanner contrast profiles.
Part III

Quantifying the Unknown

Medical AI must not overconfidently guess. Lumen CXR implements **Monte Carlo (MC) Dropout**. During inference, the system runs 20 parallel passes through the model with active dropout. By measuring the variance (predictive entropy) across these passes, we quantify the model's confidence. If the case falls below the confidence threshold, the pipeline automatically routes the case for human review.

Clinical Safety Net

Active case routing flags out-of-distribution patterns (such as surgical artifacts or foreign objects) that the model cannot safely identify, keeping the diagnosis process secure.
Part IV

An Explainable Eye

A diagnosis without explanation is a clinical black box. Lumen CXR integrates **Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping)**. Every scan dynamically extracts model activations from the last conv layer, rendering an attention heatmap showing *exactly* where the model focused. Clinicians can immediately correlate the heatmap with anatomical features (e.g. pleural margins or cardiac borders).

Clinical Trust

By comparing model activations with localized pathologies, radiologists verify that predictions are based on anatomical abnormalities rather than extraneous image attributes.
