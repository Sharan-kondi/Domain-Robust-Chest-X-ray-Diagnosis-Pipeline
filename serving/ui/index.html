Bridging the Medical AI Border: Domain-Robust Diagnostic Co-Pilots.

Welcome to the future of clinical imaging safety. Lumen CXR is a state-of-the-art diagnostic workbench that adapts neural networks across hospital borders, quantifies confidence with Monte Carlo Dropout, audits reports using self-correcting LLM agents, and grounds findings in ACR appropriateness criteria.

Part I

The Invisible Scanner Border

When deep learning meets the real world, hospital differences shouldn't break clinical trust. Different scanners, calibration settings, and patient demographics trigger invisible covariate domain shifts where traditional AI fail silently. Lumen CXR was built to shatter this barrier.

Clinical Aspect

A model trained solely on high-contrast US hospital images will misinterpret the lower-contrast images of a field clinic, mistaking machine noise for active pathology like pneumonia or consolidations.
Part II

Aligning the Covariance

We don't just hope for generalization—we mathematically enforce it. Using Correlation Alignment (CORAL), our backbone network aligns second-order statistical covariances between research clinics and local scanner baselines, filtering out scanner noise to focus exclusively on clinical anatomy.

Mathematical Alignment

By penalizing the distance between source and target feature covariance matrices, features represent clinical geometry rather than scanner contrast profiles.
Part III

Quantifying the Unknown

An AI that never doubts is a hazard. Lumen CXR deploys Monte Carlo (MC) Dropout to execute 20 randomized forward passes. By measuring predictive entropy, we establish a dynamic clinical safety net. When the model is uncertain, the system automatically routes the report for human reviewer escalation.

Clinical Safety Net

Active case routing flags out-of-distribution patterns (such as surgical artifacts or foreign objects) that the model cannot safely identify, keeping the diagnosis process secure.
Part IV

An Explainable Eye

A diagnosis without explanation is a clinical black box. Lumen CXR integrates **Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping)**. Every scan dynamically extracts model activations from the last conv layer, rendering an attention heatmap showing *exactly* where the model focused. Clinicians can immediately correlate the heatmap with anatomical features (e.g. pleural margins or cardiac borders).

Clinical Trust

By comparing model activations with localized pathologies, radiologists verify that predictions are based on anatomical abnormalities rather than extraneous image attributes.
